## Reading / Listening

Heavy diet of AI infrastructure writing lately. A cluster of pieces on KV caching and persistent attention state, Anthropic's managed agents architecture, and the 12-factor-agents repo have been shaping how I think about building reliable systems on top of LLMs. The pricing-war piece from Superframeworks is worth a read if you're building anything where inference cost is a variable.

On the more skeptical end: Val Town's "Slow Mode" post and a few pieces on tacit knowledge and agentic coding failure modes. The throughline is that AI lowers the cost of producing code but not the cost of owning it. Engineering judgment is still the bottleneck.

Still working through Chip Huyen's *AI Engineering*. Dense and practical in equal measure.
