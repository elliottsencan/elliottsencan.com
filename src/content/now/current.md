## Building

The Same Same But Different festival app is the main focus right now. It's a backend-first platform for a ~7,500-person music festival with a hard deadline of August 24. Currently in the maps and notifications milestone, working toward a mobile ship and attendee portal. Everything has to be solid before doors open.

The portfolio site is in parallel, mid-way through building out an eval layer for the LLM-wiki pipeline. Citation-faithfulness eval shipped recently; synthesis-quality is next. The open question is whether per-claim sidecar scores actually catch the failure modes I see by eye when the wiki recompiles, and I don't have a clean answer yet.

Advising at ShopTickets.net on the side. Mostly architecture conversations and code review, not day-to-day implementation.

## Reading / Listening

Heavy on AI agent architecture this week. The piece on agents needing deterministic control flow over elaborate prompt chains is sitting with me, and the SysMoBench TLA+ benchmarks (near-perfect syntax, ~46% conformance) are a good reminder that "can generate it" and "understands it" are different claims. The AI control plane framing is useful context for the ShopTickets advisory work.

Also read Scott Galloway's take that the AI job apocalypse is a narrative engineered by hyperscalers to attract capital, not an evidence-based forecast. Jevon's paradox as counterargument is well-trodden, but the capital-capture framing is sharper than most versions of this argument.

Still working through Chip Huyen's *AI Engineering*. Dense and practical in equal measure.
