The startup ecosystem is best understood not as a collection of successes but as an accumulated record of attempts. [Startups.RIP](/reading/2026-04/2026-04-30t231537-startupsrip) catalogs over 1,700 dead YC companies and argues that the ideas themselves outlive the companies that first tried them. Failure is not terminal for a concept, only for a particular execution at a particular moment.

The structural conditions that determine viability keep shifting. The collapse in AI inference pricing documented by [Ayush Chaturvedi](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-31t072101-the-ai-model-pricing-war-is-here-and-your-margins-depend-on) — a 75x spread between cheapest and most expensive frontier models — has made business models like freemium and consumer-priced AI viable that were simply unprofitable at 2025 rates. Building provider-agnostic from the start is now a structural requirement, not an optimization.

But cheaper tokens do not eliminate risk; they shift it. [Christoph Spörk](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-03t103944-the-lobster-in-the-hot-pot) warns that companies building on LLM workflows may be accruing cognitive debt, eroding institutional knowledge while an NVIDIA-driven investment bubble keeps prices artificially low. A cost shock when that bubble corrects could strand companies whose workflows depend entirely on cheap inference.

At the infrastructure layer, [a16z's profile of SpaceX](/reading/2026-06/2026-06-21t231454-spacex-and-the-sentient-sun) illustrates how reusability and revenue stacking from Starlink create the foundation for genuinely new market categories. The pattern — build infrastructure economics first, then unlock downstream markets — applies well beyond aerospace.

What makes any of this worth building returns to the product question. [Paul Buchheit](/reading/2026-06/2026-06-22t170134-if-your-product-is-great-it-doesnt-need-to-be-good) argues that success comes from being exceptional on two or three dimensions and ignoring the rest. Feature completeness has never been the standard; irreplaceability on a narrow axis is.
