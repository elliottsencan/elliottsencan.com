The startup ecosystem is easier to study through its failures than its successes. [Startups.RIP](/reading/2026-04/2026-04-30t231537-startupsrip) catalogs over 1,700 dead YC companies alongside retrospectives and rebuild playbooks, arguing that failed startup ideas outlive the companies that first pursued them. The implication is practical: the graveyard is a research library, not just a memorial.

Survival increasingly depends on correctly reading infrastructure costs before they shift. The 75x pricing gap between cheap and expensive AI models has collapsed the floor on token costs, opening business models, freemium, consumer pricing, bulk APIs, that were unworkable at 2025 rates [Chaturvedi](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-31t072101-the-ai-model-pricing-war-is-here-and-your-margins-depend-on). But [Spörk](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-03t103944-the-lobster-in-the-hot-pot) warns that cheap tokens now may be a trap: if the current AI investment cycle is a circular NVIDIA-driven bubble, a cost shock when token prices surge will cripple companies that built deep LLM dependency into their workflows and lost the institutional knowledge to operate without it.

Product strategy sits underneath all of this. [Buchheit](/reading/2026-06/2026-06-22t170134-if-your-product-is-great-it-doesnt-need-to-be-good) argues that great products nail two or three core attributes and deliberately ignore everything else. Feature completeness is neither necessary nor sufficient; Gmail and the iPod succeeded by being exceptional on a narrow axis. That framing applies pressure on founders to decide which axis matters before pricing, infrastructure, or distribution choices lock them in.

At the far end of the ambition spectrum, [Andreessen and McGuiness](/reading/2026-06/2026-06-21t231454-spacex-and-the-sentient-sun) profile SpaceX as a case study in stacking compounding advantages: Starlink revenue funds Falcon 9 reusability, which funds Starship cost reductions, which funds Mars ambitions. The lesson for the broader ecosystem is that infrastructure built for one purpose can become the financial foundation for something much larger.
