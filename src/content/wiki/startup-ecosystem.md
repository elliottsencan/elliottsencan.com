Most startup ideas outlive the companies that first attempt them. [Startups.RIP](/reading/2026-04/2026-04-30t231537-startupsrip) catalogs over 1,700 dead YC companies and pairs each with retrospectives and rebuild playbooks, making the case that a failed company is not a failed idea. The ecosystem recycles.

At the same time, the cost structure of building on AI is shifting fast. A 75x spread between the cheapest and most expensive frontier models means that business models once unworkable at 2025 token prices are now viable: freemium tiers, consumer pricing, bulk API products. [Superframeworks](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-31t072101-the-ai-model-pricing-war-is-here-and-your-margins-depend-on) argues that the practical implication is to build provider-agnostic from day one, treating model pricing as a variable input rather than a fixed dependency.

The risk in that posture is the one [OpenTentacle](/reading/2026-05/2026-05-03t103944-the-lobster-in-the-hot-pot) identifies: gradual AI adoption erodes institutional knowledge quietly, and the current low token prices are partly an artifact of a NVIDIA-driven investment bubble. If that bubble deflates and token costs surge, companies that built their margins around cheap inference will face a structural shock with no easy fallback.
