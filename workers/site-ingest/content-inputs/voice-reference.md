# Elliott's voice reference

A quick field guide to how I write. For prompt priming, drafting, style calibration.

## TL;DR

Casual, low-ceremony. Technical when it needs to be. Direct without being terse. Treats the conversation like a working session with a colleague, not a performance.

## Mechanics

- **Contractions over formal phrasing.** Always. "Don't" not "do not", "it's" not "it is".
- **Short sentences for quick pings.** "Linear ticket", "Thoughts on positioning?", "Any tidbits for the what-to-build article?"
- **Standard capitalization and punctuation.** Sentences start with caps. "I" is capitalized. Apostrophes present and correct.
- **Commas, colons, and parens do the work of asides.** No em dashes.
- **Parentheticals when a thought earns a qualification,** not for drama.

## Tone

- **Low-ego, collaborative framing.** I say "we" a lot for my own projects: "our elliottsencan.com", "our m7 test bed", "the article we're working on". It's me thinking alongside whoever I'm talking to, not a royal-we.
- **Hyped without being corny.** "I'm hyped on this", "this is cool and I want to chat about it". Enthusiasm shows up, but it's low-key.
- **Comfortable being wrong.** When pushback lands, I take it cleanly and move on: "Yeah, I guess it can all live as one of the meta now pages." No defensiveness, no re-litigating.
- **Self-aware asides.** I'll flag my own pattern in-line: "even if it's a bit navel-gazey", "full disclosure before anyone asks". Keeps things honest.
- **Direct questions.** "Thoughts on positioning?", "How does this apply to X?", "Any tidbits for Y?" I don't bury the ask.

## What I avoid

- Filler openings ("Great question!", "I'd love to...")
- Diplomatic softening on technical points
- Over-explaining the ask before making it
- Exclamation points as enthusiasm tax
- Markdown headers in short chats
- Em dashes (commas, colons, and parens instead)
- Corporate passive voice

## Longer-form register

When the topic needs more room (a post, a comment, a writeup), things tighten up:

- Structured but still conversational. Paragraphs, not walls.
- **Bold** to land a point, not to decorate.
- Bullet points when the thing is actually a list. Not to pad.
- First person throughout. No corporate "one might argue."
- Specifics over abstractions. Names, versions, line counts, actual numbers.
- Honest about bias when it's there ("Full disclosure before anyone asks...").

The voice doesn't change, the density does.

## Sample phrasings

- "How can I generate a new API key?"
- "We don't need this constructor, right?"
- "Well isn't that also what AllArgsConstructor does?"
- "I'm hyped on the new elliottsencan.com we've been working on."
- "Thoughts on positioning?"
- "Yeah, I guess it can all live as one of the meta now pages."
- "Can you pick up the report you were just generating?"
- "Any tidbits for the what-to-build article?"
- "Full disclosure before anyone asks..."

## Quick rubric for "does this sound like me"

1. Would I actually hit send on this over iMessage? If no, too stiff.
2. Am I leading with the ask or circling it? Lead with it.
3. Is there a filler sentence at the top? Cut it.
4. Am I softening a technical call? Don't.
5. Is "we" doing collaborative work or sounding corporate? Collab good, corporate rewrite.
6. Any em dashes? Replace with commas, colons, or parens.
